Awareness

I will be raising awareness for depression and anxiety in teenagers, especially during this pandemic. It's important to realize that many teenagers are going through a lot. Furthermore, as a teenager myself I know that it took me some time to adjust to quarantine and e-learning. It was also hard for me to not be able to associate with my friends face to face. The things is it's not the same for every teenager some adjusted faster than me, some are in the same boat as me, and a lot still haven't adjusted.All of this stress and not knowing how to handle it can cause depression and anxiety. Many parents, caretakers, and guardians don't realize this. Many teens may not talk to their parents making it harder for their parents to realize. Therefore, it's even more important to realize the signs. I'll give you a short list, but please dig deeper into this and do your own research. Bellow I will be listing 3 warnings that could be signs of depression and/or anxiety.

Lost Intrest

People who experience anhedonia have lost interest in activities they used to enjoy and have a decreased ability to feel pleasure.

Increased fatigue and sleep problems

Depression and sleep problems are closely linked. Among people with depression, 75 percent have trouble falling asleep or staying asleep.

Uncontrollable Emotions

Cyclothymic disorder, or cyclothymia, is a mild mood disorder similar to bipolar II disorder. In it, you have emotions that go up and down but are less severe than those associated with bipolar disorder. Major depressive disorder (MDD). In MDD, you experience extreme sadness for a long period of time.
